Notable Work

+ Equal Pay Act of 1963: Ginsburg co-founded the Women's Rights Project at the ACLU, where she played a crucial role in the passage of this landmark legislation. The act prohibits wage discrimination based on gender, ensuring fair pay for women in the workforce.

+ Civil Rights Act of 1964: As a key advocate, Ginsburg fought to include a clause in the act that prohibited sex-based discrimination. This addition was instrumental in advancing gender equality in areas like employment and education.

+ Reproductive Rights Advocacy: Throughout her career, Ginsburg ardently defended a woman's right to choose, advocating for reproductive rights and access to contraception, notably in cases like Planned Parenthood v. Casey and Whole Woman's Health v. Hellerstedt.

+ Lilly Ledbetter Fair Pay Act of 2009: In a significant legislative victory, Ginsburg's dissent in Ledbetter v. Goodyear Tire & Rubber Co. prompted Congress to pass this act, easing the statute of limitations for filing pay discrimination lawsuits and bolstering pay equity protections.
